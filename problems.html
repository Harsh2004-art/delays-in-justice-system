What’s Going Wrong?

The core problems that cause delays in our justice system.

Key Problems in the Justice System

1. Huge Backlog

Lakhs of cases are pending for years at different levels of courts, putting pressure on judges and staff.

2. Manual Paperwork

Physical files, handwritten notes, and manual registers slow down filing, tracking, and updating cases.

3. Slow Case Tracking

Parties struggle to know the exact status of their case, next hearing date, or previous orders easily.

4. Frequent Adjournments

Hearings are postponed repeatedly due to lack of coordination, absence of parties, or missing documents.

5. No Smart Reminders

There are no automatic reminders for upcoming hearings for lawyers, parties, or even staff.

6. Poor Use of Technology

Many courts still depend on manual systems instead of digital dashboards and automated workflows.

Impact on Citizens

Because of these problems, cases take longer to finish, citizens lose trust, and the principle of “Justice delayed is justice denied” becomes a reality.
